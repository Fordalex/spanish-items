Word classes

  • Noun - Names a person, place, thing, or idea (e.g., dog, city, happiness)
  • Pronoun - Replaces a noun to avoid repetition (e.g., he, she, it, they)
  • Verb - Describes an action or state (e.g., eat, drink, run, walk)
  • Adjective - Describes or modifies a noun (e.g., big, small, happy, sad)
  • Adverb - Modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb (e.g., quickly, very, well)
  • Preposition - Shows the relationship of a noun or pronoun to another word (e.g., after, before, over, under)
  • Conjunction - Connects words, phrases, or clauses (e.g., and, but, or, because)
  • Interjection - Expresses strong emotion or reaction (e.g., wow, ouch, hey, oh)
